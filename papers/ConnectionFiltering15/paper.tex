\begin{abstract}
This paper studies communication patterns in mobile applications.
Our experience shows that 65\% of communication performed via HTTP, socket and RPC connection statement encoded in
top-popular Android applications from Google Play can be deemed non-essential: these connections do not have any effect on the user-observable application functionality. 
We explore properties of such ``hidden'' connections, concluding that neither type nor the communication destination are indicative for the connection being non-essential. 
Studying syntactic patterns of these connections lead us to devising a static analysis technique that 
is able to detect those with 84\% -90\% precision and 63\%-64\% recall, depending on whether advertisement is treated as  essential or not.
